INTRAMUROS

Intramuros, Manila, is a historical gem that beckons you to step back in time and explore the rich tapestry of its past. This walled city, located in the heart of Manila, offers a captivating journey through centuries of history, culture, and architecture. Discover the wonders of Intramuros, where every cobblestone tells a story.

History of Intramuros

Intramuros served as the seat of government of the Captaincy General of the Philippines, a component realm of the Spanish Empire, housing the colony's governor-general from its founding in 1571 until 1865, and the Real Audiencia of Manila until the end of Spanish rule during the Philippine Revolution of 1898.

Point of Interest

Fort Santiago

Fort Santiago, a 16th-century citadel, is one of Intramuros' most iconic landmarks. It served as a military fortress and later as a prison. Explore its dark dungeons and beautiful gardens.

San Agustin Church
